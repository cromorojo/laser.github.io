LASER TYPES FOR TATTOO REMOVAL
Q-Switched Nd:YAG (1064nm)
Black, blue, dark tattoos. Safe for all skin types including darker skin (Fitzpatrick IV-VI)
Q-Switched Nd:YAG (532nm)
Red, orange, yellow pigments. Best for lighter skin types (Fitzpatrick I-III)
Q-Switched Alexandrite (755nm)
Green, blue, black pigments. Moderate penetration depth
Q-Switched Ruby (694nm)
Green, blue pigments. Light skin only (Fitzpatrick I-II)
Picosecond Lasers
All colors with superior clearance. 30-50% faster treatment, fewer sessions required
TREATMENT INTERVALS & EXPECTATIONS
Tattoo Factor Sessions Interval
Amateur/Small (<2 inches) 3-6 6-8 weeks
Professional/Medium (2-6 inches) 6-10 8-12 weeks
Dense/Multicolor (>6 inches) 10-15+ 12+ weeks
Cover-up Tattoos 15-20+ 12+ weeks
Cosmetic Tattoos 8-12 6-8 weeks

Variables: size, age, colors, depth, location, immune response, skin type

INK COLORS & REMOVAL DIFFICULTY
EASIEST TO REMOVE (1-3 sessions difference)
Black, dark blue, dark green - excellent absorption at 1064nm
MODERATE DIFFICULTY (3-5 additional sessions)
Red, orange, purple - requires 532nm wavelength
MOST DIFFICULT (5-8 additional sessions)
Yellow, light blue, light green, fluorescent colors
PROBLEMATIC INKS (May require specialist referral)
Metallic inks (may darken), white ink (may turn gray), cosmetic tattoos with iron oxide
FACTORS AFFECTING TREATMENT SUCCESS

Positive Factors (Faster Clearance):

  • Recent tattoo (<2 years old)
  • Simple black or dark blue ink
  • Amateur/homemade tattoo
  • Healthy immune system
  • Non-smoker status
  • Tattoo on extremities (arms/legs)
  • Light skin tone (Fitzpatrick I-II)
  • Small size (<2 square inches)
  • Regular exercise and good circulation
  • Age under 40 years

Negative Factors (Slower Clearance):

  • Old tattoo (>10 years)
  • Multiple bright colors
  • Professional/dense tattoo
  • Compromised immune system
  • Smoker (reduces healing by 50%)
  • Tattoo on torso or face
  • Dark skin tone (Fitzpatrick V-VI)
  • Large size (>6 square inches)
  • Poor circulation conditions
  • Age over 65 years
PRE-TREATMENT ASSESSMENT & PREPARATION

Essential Assessment Checklist:

  • Fitzpatrick skin type classification (I-VI)
  • Tattoo documentation: age, size, location, colors
  • Previous tattoo work or removal attempts
  • Medical history review and current medications
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding status (females)
  • Recent sun exposure or tanning
  • Active skin conditions in treatment area
  • Realistic expectation setting and consent
  • Photography for treatment records
  • Test patch if indicated (sensitive areas)

Pre-Treatment Instructions:

  • No sun exposure 4 weeks before treatment
  • Discontinue self-tanning products 2 weeks prior
  • Avoid aspirin/anti-inflammatories 1 week before
  • Ensure area is clean and hair-free
  • No topical anesthetics without approval
  • Arrange transport if topical anesthesia used
  • Wear loose, comfortable clothing
  • Stay hydrated and eat before treatment
  • Inform of any illness or medication changes
  • Complete updated consent forms
TREATMENT PROCESS & PROTOCOL
Step Action Key Points Safety Considerations
1 Patient Preparation Position comfortably, ensure eye protection, apply topical anesthetic if required Verify identity, check consent, confirm no contraindications
2 Laser Setup Select appropriate wavelength, set fluence and spot size, calibrate cooling Test fire, check emergency stops, ensure protective equipment
3 Test Patch Start conservative, assess immediate tissue response Watch for excessive blanching, bleeding, or immediate adverse reaction
4 Treatment Systematic coverage, 10-20% overlap, consistent technique Monitor skin response, adjust parameters as needed, pain management
5 Immediate Post-Care Cool area, apply protective dressing, document treatment Check for adverse reactions, provide aftercare instructions
6 Follow-up Schedule next appointment, progress assessment Review healing, address concerns, modify treatment plan if needed
CONTRAINDICATIONS & PRECAUTIONS

ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS:

Pregnancy & Breastfeeding: No treatment during pregnancy or while breastfeeding
Active Skin Cancer: In or near treatment area
Keloid History: Previous keloid formation in treatment area
Photosensitive Medications: Recent use of photosensitizing drugs
Recent Isotretinoin: Within 6 months of Accutane use

RELATIVE CONTRAINDICATIONS:

Immunosuppression: Assess healing capacity and infection risk
Diabetes: Evaluate wound healing and circulation
Recent Sun Exposure: Tanned skin increases complication risk
Unrealistic Expectations: Ensure proper counseling completed
Age Under 18: Requires parental consent and special consideration
COMPLICATIONS RECOGNITION & MANAGEMENT
ANAPHYLAXIS / SEVERE ALLERGIC REACTION
Difficulty breathing, widespread rash, swelling, rapid pulse, loss of consciousness
ACTION: Call 999 immediately. Administer adrenaline if trained. Maintain airway. Document time and symptoms.
SEVERE BURNS / FULL THICKNESS INJURY
White/charred skin, no sensation, immediate deep tissue damage
ACTION: Call 999. Cool with saline (not ice). Cover with sterile dressing. Do not remove debris. Pain management.
SIGNIFICANT INFECTION / CELLULITIS
Spreading redness, warmth, red streaking, fever, lymph node swelling, systemic illness
ACTION: Same-day GP/hospital referral. Swab for culture. Consider IV antibiotics. Document progression.
DELAYED HEALING / NON-HEALING WOUNDS
No healing progress after 2 weeks, increasing pain, unusual discharge
ACTION: Medical review within 24-48 hours. Assess for underlying conditions. Wound care specialist referral.
TEXTURAL CHANGES / SCARRING
Raised/depressed scars, texture changes, atrophic scarring developing
ACTION: Dermatology referral. Document with photography. Consider treatment modification/cessation.
HYPERPIGMENTATION / HYPOPIGMENTATION
Darkening or lightening of treated skin, usually temporary
ACTION: Reassure patient (usually resolves 6-12 months). Sun protection essential. Monitor progress.
BLISTERING (EXPECTED RESPONSE)
Clear fluid-filled blisters within 24-48 hours post-treatment
ACTION: Normal response. Keep clean and dry. Do not pop blisters. Protective dressing if needed.
PARADOXICAL DARKENING
Temporary darkening of treated tattoo, especially cosmetic tattoos with iron oxide
ACTION: Explain to patient. Usually resolves with subsequent treatments. Modify parameters if needed.
POST-TREATMENT CARE PROTOCOL

IMMEDIATE CARE (First 24-48 Hours):

  • Apply ice packs for 10-15 minutes every hour
  • Keep area clean and dry
  • Apply prescribed topical antibiotic if indicated
  • Use non-adherent dressing if blistering occurs
  • Take paracetamol for pain (avoid aspirin/ibuprofen)
  • No hot showers or baths on treated area
  • Avoid tight clothing over treatment site
  • No exercise for 24-48 hours

ONGOING CARE (Until Next Session):

  • Apply SPF 30+ sunscreen daily (even if covered)
  • Keep area moisturized with gentle, fragrance-free lotion
  • No picking, scratching, or rubbing treated area
  • Avoid swimming pools, hot tubs, saunas for 2 weeks
  • No self-tanning products on treated area
  • Return to normal activities gradually
  • Contact clinic if signs of infection develop
  • Attend follow-up appointments as scheduled

⚠️ WHEN TO SEEK IMMEDIATE MEDICAL ATTENTION

Contact emergency services (999) or attend A&E immediately if experiencing:

  • Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Widespread rash or severe swelling
  • Signs of severe infection (fever, red streaking, rapid pulse)
  • Severe pain not controlled by prescribed medication
  • Any signs of serious allergic reaction
EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS & CONTACT INFORMATION

🚨 EMERGENCY ACTION PLAN

STEP 1: IMMEDIATE RESPONSE
  • Assess patient condition
  • Call 999 if life-threatening
  • Provide first aid as trained
  • Do not leave patient alone
STEP 2: DOCUMENTATION
  • Record time and symptoms
  • Note treatment parameters used
  • Take photographs if appropriate
  • Complete incident report
STEP 3: FOLLOW-UP
  • Inform senior practitioner
  • Contact insurance provider
  • Schedule follow-up care
  • Review and learn from incident

EMERGENCY SUPPLIES CHECKLIST:

  • Adrenaline auto-injector (if trained)
  • Oxygen and bag-valve mask
  • Sterile saline for irrigation
  • Non-adherent dressings
  • Emergency medication list
  • Patient emergency contact forms
