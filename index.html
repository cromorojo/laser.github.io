ABSOLUTE CONTRAINDICATIONS

Contraindicated
Malignant Melanoma
Appearance
Dark, irregular mole with asymmetry, border irregularity, color variation, diameter >6mm (ABCDE criteria)
Critical Risk
Laser energy can stimulate melanoma cells and cause metastasis. Any suspicious lesions must be biopsied before treatment.
Protocol
NEVER treat over or near suspicious moles. Refer immediately to dermatologist for evaluation and biopsy.
Contraindicated
Active Herpes Outbreak
Appearance
Painful vesicles, blisters, or ulcers typically around mouth, lips, or genitals with burning sensation
Treatment Risk
Laser treatment during active outbreak worsens infection, delays healing, and significantly increases scarring risk.
Protocol
Wait until outbreak completely resolves (7-14 days). Consider prophylactic antiviral therapy for recurrent cases.
Contraindicated
Keloid Scarring History
Appearance
Raised, thickened scar tissue extending beyond original wound boundaries, often with continued growth
Risk Assessment
Laser energy triggers excessive collagen production, worsening keloid formation and creating permanent disfigurement.
Alternative Options
Consider chemical peels, dermabrasion, or surgical excision with radiation therapy by specialist only.
Contraindicated
Pregnancy & Breastfeeding
Patient Status
Confirmed pregnancy at any stage or active breastfeeding period
Safety Concerns
Unknown effects of laser energy on fetal development. Pigment particles released may cross placental barrier.
Waiting Period
Wait until after delivery and cessation of breastfeeding (minimum 3 months post-weaning recommended).
Contraindicated
Active Skin Cancer
Types
Squamous cell carcinoma, basal cell carcinoma, or any malignancy in or near treatment area
Treatment Risk
Laser energy can interfere with cancer treatment protocols and potentially stimulate tumor growth or spread.
Required Action
Complete cancer treatment first. Obtain written oncologist clearance before considering tattoo removal.
⚠️

PROCEED WITH CAUTION

Caution Required
Eczema/Atopic Dermatitis
Appearance
Red, inflamed patches with intense itching, dryness, possible crusting or weeping areas
Treatment Challenges
Increased risk of post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, delayed healing, condition flare-ups triggered by laser trauma.
Modified Protocol
Reduce fluence by 20-30%, extend intervals to 8-10 weeks, pre-treat with topical corticosteroids, intensive moisturizing regimen.
Caution Required
Psoriasis
Appearance
Red, raised plaques with thick silvery scales, commonly on elbows, knees, scalp, and lower back
Koebner Phenomenon
Laser trauma can trigger new psoriatic lesions at treatment site (Koebner phenomenon). May worsen existing plaques.
Treatment Strategy
Treat only during stable/remission phase. Reduce fluence by 25%. Consider topical therapy pre/post treatment. Monitor closely.
Caution Required
Vitiligo
Appearance
Well-demarcated white patches with complete loss of pigmentation, often symmetrical distribution
Pigmentation Risks
Risk of triggering new vitiligo patches, unpredictable response in adjacent normal skin, permanent hypopigmentation.
Special Protocol
Avoid treating through vitiligo patches. Use conservative settings on surrounding skin. Set realistic expectations about pigment outcomes.
Caution Required
Recent Isotretinoin Use
Patient History
Accutane/isotretinoin use within past 6-12 months, regardless of dosage or duration
Healing Complications
Significantly impaired wound healing, increased scarring risk, abnormal collagen response, delayed epithelialization.
Waiting Protocol
Wait minimum 6 months after last dose. Consider 12 months for high-dose or long-term use. Mandatory patch testing.
Caution Required
Hypertrophic Scarring Tendency
History Indicators
Previous raised scars, slow healing history, excessive scar tissue formation, family history of poor healing
Scarring Risk
Higher risk of textural changes, raised treatment areas, permanent skin alterations, patient dissatisfaction.
Risk Mitigation
Ultra-conservative settings, longer healing intervals, silicone gel post-treatment, extensive patch testing, frequent monitoring.

MEDICAL CLEARANCE REQUIRED

Medical Clearance
Diabetes Mellitus
Patient Condition
Type 1 or Type 2 diabetes, especially with poor glycemic control (HbA1c >7%)
Healing Complications
Delayed wound healing, increased infection risk, poor tissue repair, peripheral neuropathy affecting sensation.
Medical Requirements
Recent HbA1c <7%, endocrinologist clearance, careful monitoring for infection signs, extended healing protocols.
Medical Clearance
Autoimmune Disorders
Conditions Included
Lupus, rheumatoid arthritis, scleroderma, autoimmune thyroid disease, inflammatory bowel disease
Treatment Risks
Unpredictable healing patterns, potential to trigger disease flares, medication interactions affecting skin response.
Specialist Requirements
Rheumatologist/specialist written clearance required. May need temporary medication modifications under medical supervision.
Medical Clearance
Blood Clotting Disorders
Conditions
Hemophilia, thrombocytopenia, anticoagulant therapy (warfarin, heparin, DOACs)
Bleeding Risks
Excessive bleeding during/after treatment, hematoma formation, prolonged healing, bruising complications.
Hematology Protocol
Recent coagulation studies, possible medication timing adjustment, emergency bleeding protocols, pressure dressing supplies.
Medical Clearance
Cardiac Devices
Device Types
Implanted pacemaker, defibrillator (ICD), cardiac resynchronization therapy devices
Electromagnetic Risk
Laser systems may interfere with device function, potentially causing malfunction, inappropriate shocks, or rhythm disturbances.
Cardiology Protocol
Cardiologist written approval required. Device interrogation pre/post treatment. Emergency cardiac protocols must be available.
Medical Clearance
Immunosuppression
Patient Categories
Organ transplant recipients, chemotherapy patients, chronic steroid users, HIV with low CD4 counts
Healing Complications
Severely impaired wound healing, increased infection risk, unpredictable pigment response, delayed recovery.
Alternative Approach
Consider tattoo cover-up rather than removal. If proceeding, requires infectious disease or transplant specialist clearance.

SKIN TYPE CONSIDERATIONS

High Risk
Fitzpatrick Type V-VI
Skin Characteristics
Dark brown to black skin, rarely burns, tans very easily, high melanin content
Pigmentation Risks
Very high risk of hypopigmentation, keloid formation, post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation, paradoxical darkening.
Laser Protocol
Nd:YAG 1064nm ONLY. Reduce fluence 30-50%, longer pulse durations, aggressive cooling, 10-12 week intervals minimum.
Optimal Response
Fitzpatrick Type I-II
Skin Characteristics
Very fair to fair skin, burns easily, minimal tanning ability, low melanin content
Treatment Response
Excellent response to most laser types, minimal pigmentation changes, faster clearance rates, fewer sessions required.
Optimal Settings
Multiple wavelengths effective (755nm, 1064nm, picosecond). Standard fluences well-tolerated. 6-8 week intervals.
Wait Required
Recently Tanned Skin
Skin State
Recent sun exposure, natural tanning, or artificial tanning (spray tan, tanning beds)
Treatment Complications
Increased melanin competes with tattoo pigment for laser energy, higher burn risk, unpredictable pigment changes.
Waiting Protocol
Wait 4-6 weeks for natural tan to fade, 2-3 weeks for artificial tan. Mandate strict sun protection throughout treatment.

POST-TREATMENT COMPLICATIONS

Monitor Closely
Hypopigmentation
Appearance
Permanent white or lighter patches where tattoo was removed, contrast with surrounding normal skin
Risk Factors
5-15% incidence, higher in darker skin types, over-treatment, excessive overlapping passes, aggressive parameters.
Prevention Strategy
Conservative fluences, avoid overlapping pulses, 8+ week intervals, proper endpoint assessment, test spots mandatory.
Emergency
Signs of Infection
Warning Signs
Increasing pain after 48h, purulent discharge, spreading erythema, fever, red streaking, malodor
Emergency Protocol
Immediate antibiotic therapy, wound culture, discontinue all laser treatments until complete resolution.
Immediate Action
Refer to physician same day. Document with photos. Consider hospitalization for systemic symptoms.
Monitor Closely
Textural Changes
Appearance
Raised, depressed, or rough skin texture different from surrounding tissue, permanent surface changes
Contributing Factors
Multiple treatments, high fluences, inadequate healing time, patient non-compliance with aftercare protocols.
Management
Early intervention with topical treatments, consider fractional resurfacing for established changes, realistic expectations.
🚨 EMERGENCY PROTOCOLS & IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

Severe Burns/Blistering

  • Stop treatment immediately
  • Apply cold compress (not ice directly)
  • Document with standardized photos
  • Refer to burn specialist within 24 hours
  • No topical medications without MD approval
  • Schedule follow-up within 48 hours

Infection Warning Signs

  • Increasing pain after 48 hours
  • Purulent or malodorous discharge
  • Red streaking from treatment site
  • Fever or systemic symptoms
  • Immediate physician referral required
  • Consider emergency department if severe

Allergic Reactions

  • Widespread urticaria/hives
  • Difficulty breathing or swallowing
  • Facial swelling (lips, tongue, throat)
  • Call emergency services immediately
  • Administer epinephrine if available
  • Monitor vital signs continuously

Abnormal Scarring

  • Raised tissue beyond treatment borders
  • Deep depression or skin atrophy
  • Persistent induration after 4 weeks
  • Dermatology consultation required
  • Consider silicone gel therapy
  • Document progression with photos
📋 ESSENTIAL PRE-TREATMENT ASSESSMENT PROTOCOL
  1. Complete comprehensive medical history including all medications, supplements, and recent treatments
  2. Perform thorough skin examination of treatment area and evaluate surrounding tissue health
  3. Document any suspicious lesions with photography and refer for dermatological evaluation
  4. Assess Fitzpatrick skin type and document recent sun exposure or tanning history
  5. Review previous tattoo removal treatments, complications, and healing patterns
  6. Evaluate healing from recent procedures, injuries, or infections in treatment area
  7. Photograph tattoo with standardized lighting, measurement reference, and color calibration
  8. Perform mandatory patch test with planned parameters in inconspicuous area
  9. Obtain comprehensive written informed consent after thorough risk discussion
  10. Schedule follow-up within 48-72 hours for first treatment response assessment and monitoring

Professional Disclaimer & Legal Requirements

This guide is for professional use only by qualified laser practitioners. Always obtain comprehensive medical history, perform patch testing, and maintain current professional liability insurance. When in doubt about any condition or patient suitability, refer to appropriate medical specialists.

Treatment decisions should always be individualized based on patient assessment, medical history, and clinical judgment. This guide does not replace proper medical training or professional consultation.
